\(\begin{array}{ll}
 \{ & 
\begin{array}{ll}

 \text{kx}+y+z & =-2 \\
\end{array}
 \\
\end{array}\)\\
have\\
1. No solution\\
2. A unique solution\\
3. Infinitely many solutions\\
Hint. Find the reduced echelon form of the augmented matrix, then analyze different cases (beware of division by zero!).\\
\\
\(\begin{array}{ll}
 \{ & 
\begin{array}{ll}
 x+y+\text{kz} & =1 \\

\end{array}

\end{array}

\begin{array}{cccc}

\end{array}
\right)\Rightarrow\) \(\frac{1}{k}R_1\rightarrow R_1\Rightarrow\) \(\left(
\begin{array}{cccc}

\end{array}
\right)\Rightarrow\) \(\begin{array}{c}
 R_2-R_1\rightarrow R_2 \\
 R_3-R_1\rightarrow R_3 \\
\end{array}
\Rightarrow\) \(\left(
\begin{array}{cccc}

\end{array}
\right)\Rightarrow\) \(\frac{k}{-1+k^2}R_2\rightarrow R_2\Rightarrow\) \(\left(

\end{array}
\Rightarrow\) \(\left(
\begin{array}{cccc}

\end{array}
\right)\)\\
