$dU = T dS - P dV$ $$ dA = T dS - P dV - T dS - S dT $$ $$ dA = - P dV - S dT $$ $$ \left(\frac{\partial A}{\partial V}\right)_T = -P $$

  • on the other hand, $$ A = \frac{n^2 a}{V} - n R T ln\left(V - n b\right) + J(T) $$ $$ \left(\frac{\partial A}{\partial V}\right)_T = \frac{n^2 a}{V^2} - \frac{n R T}{V - n b} $$ $\left(\frac{\partial J}{\partial V}\right)_T = 0;$ as J is a function of T only

  • therefore

    $$ -P = \frac{n^2 a}{V^2} - \frac{n R T}{V - n b} $$

Exercise 4 - Part E¶

Rubber is composed of long polymer chains. Each of the single bonds between two carbon atoms in those chaines can in principle rotate so that the chain is locally either straight or bent. There are many ways to rotate so that the chain bends but there is only one way to rotate so that the chain is straight and maximally extended. Thus entropy favors shorter bent chains. There is much less disorder when chains are straight. So there are more straight chains when the rubber is stretched, so the chains must achieve longer lengths. When the rubber is heated by increasing the temperature, it favors the free energy of structures having more entropy. At equilibrium, shorter chains are favored over longer and the rubber contracts. [3]
